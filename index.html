Labor researcher, organizer & communications strategist

I work at the intersection of industrial policy, automation, and the future of work. I study the empirics of the on-the-ground experiences of workers who build the chip economy, and how to improve workforce-development pipelines and workplace conditions so the people doing the work get the good jobs the AI era keeps promising.

I

Research

My research spans a wide breadth, centered on understanding the role of labor in defining the current historical conjuncture, both the present scope of possibilities and a path forward for collective human progress.

II

Nonprofit

In the nonprofit sector, I promote workforce-development initiatives that enable workers to best reap the benefits of the profits they make possible in the American economy. As part of this broader goal, I have also designed educational programming to bridge gaps between our current, flawed education system and a future where everyone has access to fulfilling, comprehensive instruction.
